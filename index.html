Any-clock

Passion project

Any-clock is an IoT clock-like display capable of extracting and displaying data from any website. The project began with the idea to upgrade the concept of an ordinary desk clock so that instead of a ubiquitous time display, it could present the real-time data extracted from any HTML file/website available on a selected HTTP or HTTPS server.

For this task, I designed the overall system, specified requirements, firmware structure and components, use cases and designed the hardware for the display. I focused on reliability and robustness, achieved through simplicity and thoughtful design.

Firmware was a critical component in achieving mentioned reliability, so I spent most of the time writing, testing and improving C code for the ESP32 microcontroller, which controls the device. All project files related to the project, including the code, can be found on GitHub.

HertzNet

[Open Source] BEng Thesis

HertzNet is a system for measuring mains (utility/line) frequency in different locations and aggregating collected data for use in power system analysis/research.

As part of my thesis, I designed and developed a prototype with a low-price plug-in device and cloud infrastructure. During my work, I followed a strict engineering workflow (combining the waterfall model with iterative/lean methods). I developed several hardware prototypes before arriving at the final design and similarly, I followed agile methodology in developing C (ESP-IDF) firmware (utilising Git & GitHub with CI workflows).

The measurements collected by the device continuously since June 2023 are available for download from the online dashboard and can be used for:

  • Post-disturbance event reconstructions
  • Estimating in real-time grid disturbance location
  • Visualising current wide-area system frequency

The final report with the associated demonstration received a first-class (highest) mark and the project also won the 10th edition of the Tech Master Event.

SunBoard

Passion project

During my first year at the University of Manchester, I decided to complement the theory from the lectures with a hands-on project. That's how I started the SunBoard project, as part of which I designed, built and programmed a solar charge controller for small solar panels with an MPPT algorithm and wireless control.

I developed a custom C++ (Arduino) library with a proportional controller for setting the output voltage (< 0.02 V accuracy), Vin, Vout, and Iin measurement methods (< 0.01 V accuracy), Maximum Power Point Tracking algorithm and wireless capabilities (reporting the state of the board over Bluetooth). Project files can be found on my GitHub account.

As part of the project, I also designed and assembled a bespoke PCB with a DC/DC Buck converter (70% efficiency) based on AVR ATmega328 MCU. In June 2021, the project was awarded at the RS Student Project Competition.

Spacein180

Passion project

Space in 180 ° is a project I carried out at the end of middle school (16 y.o.) during which I developed and successfully sent an amateur research probe to the stratosphere (to an altitude of about 30 km). It was equipped with homemade hardware, e.g. AVR-based (C++ firmware) logging module with an SD card, sensors, and a range of cameras. To lift the instruments, I used a 2-meter-wide meteorological balloon filled with helium.

I financed the necessary equipment from a successful crowdfunding campaign with simultaneous support from cooperating companies and sponsors.

Throughout that year, I had to coordinate the crowdfunding campaign and the development of hardware and firmware for the probe. I also led a small team of people and companies which helped me in this undertaking. During and after the project I had the chance to make multiple public appearances and presentations about Space in 180 °, including one at the Poznan University of Technology (PUT). 

The project gained considerable media publicity, and information about it appeared in the leading Polish radio and television stations, e.g. TVN24, Glos Wielkopolski, WP.pl, Polsat News, Polskie Radio, TVN Turbo. The PDF project summary can be found here.

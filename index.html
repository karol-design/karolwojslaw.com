Ovon Technology

Intern [Embedded Systems]

Ovon Technology is an IoT-focused consulting company from Manchester. They design electronics and firmware for external clients but also develop a range of products as part of their brand "Ovon Home" concentrated around intelligent heating (radiator and room thermostats, boiler controllers). During my internship, I worked with Espressif ESP32 MCU and ESP-IDF. I wrote and modified firmware in C and tracked my work with Git repositories (hosted on GitHub). I also used DMM, oscilloscope, logic analyzer and adjustable power supply on a daily basis. My tasks were both firmware and hardware-related, and a number of them are listed below:
  • Developing proofs-of-concept, prototypes and presentation demo setups (e.g. building a WiFi Hub to control multiple BLE radiator thermostats via MQTT (and Alexa's voice commands - through AWS scripts); creating a simple SPI-based driver and testing the new BLDC motor driver IC, developing Infrared (IrDA) data exchange modules for the RF Shield Box).
  • Testing new firmware functionalities (e.g. ESP32-C3 Low Power Modes, ESP32 BLE Mesh capabilities).
  • Ordering parts (based on BOM), soldering (THT/SMD: soldering iron, hot air rework station, reflow oven), assembling and testing new board designs.
  • Creating boiler test setups (EBUS/OpenTherm Protocols, sensors, boiler behaviour simulation).

Sportfood

Prototype developer

At the time I began working at Sportfood (now Fitly), the company focused on the production of healthy drinks and meals, which they sold using a few dozen modified display refrigerators (with no control over how much each customer takes out, and what is the current inventory size).

I joined Sportfood at the invitation of its owner, who asked me to conduct research and a feasibility study on how the system can be improved to make it more reliable and scalable. I identified potential solutions, created simple prototypes (using Arduino, various sensors and finally also RFID modules), then established cooperation with an RFID consulting company from Warsaw and supervised tests, which resulted in the preparation of a working prototype (vending refrigerator, capable of identifying up to 120 products placed inside).

Openspace Foundation

Founder & project leader

In 2018, I formed the Openspace Foundation with its first primary project - Opensat - aimed at developing and launching into space a student-made small satellite (cubesat) to test flexible perovskite photovoltaic panels in low Earth orbit. After about a year, a 60-page long preliminary mission concept (MDR) was produced, however, the project had to be suspended due to the lack of funds and the technical challenges exceeding the team's capabilities. My responsibilities in the project included managing the team and workflow, establishing cooperation with companies, and writing the MDR.

Following the termination of the Opensat project, I began another initiative, this time focused on the promotion of STEM subjects, amongst kids in primary schools. I planned and conducted the "Space Fridays" project in three different schools in Warsaw, with full-day workshops in each of them (e.g. building simple Arduino rovers or satellite models with LEDs and batteries). At the end of each "Space Friday," a weather balloon was released to a few hundred meters carrying a probe prepared during the workshops.

In total, over 400 children took part in workshops and presentations organised by the foundation. To carry out all of the planned activities, I hired and coordinated two supporting tutors (from the Warsaw University of Technology), and to finance the project, I applied for and received a grant from the Warsaw City Council.
